📝 NCM Description Guidelines

Enter the NCM description in a "should be / is" format to clearly articulate the discrepancy.

For NCMs not related to product discrepancies (e.g., Calibration, Documentation), provide sufficient detail to ensure the intent and scope are clearly understood.

Important: Ensure you select the correct NCM Type that corresponds to the issue (Calibration, Return, Rework, etc.).

🏷️ Subject Codes

Select the subject code that best describes the nature of the non-conformance. If other code is needed, coordinate with the Quality Manager to ensure it is added to the system.

  • HFS – Held For Statement

    Use for documentation and administrative issues including typos.

  • DAM – Damaged

    Use for physical damage, including scratches and surface defects.

  • FRM – Form

    Use for geometric dimensioning and tolerance (GDT) issues such as Position, Perpendicularity, and Profile.

  • FIT – Fit

    Use for fit and interference issues between components.

  • FUN – Function

    Use for functional performance and operational failures.

  • WKM – Workmanship

    Use for workmanship issues not covered by form/fit/function subjects (e.g., burrs, contamination, assembly errors).

🔧 CAL Type Subject Codes

For Calibration (CAL) type NCMs, use these specific subject codes to indicate the nature of the calibration issue.

  • OOT – Out of Tolerance

    Use when a calibration discrepancy indicates the equipment or instrument is operating outside acceptable tolerance limits.

  • EOL – End of Life

    Use when a calibrated instrument has reached its end of life or is no longer suitable for use and requires retirement or replacement (Eg: Hardness block used up).

🔍 Root Cause Codes

Identify the root cause of the non-conformance. If the cause is undetermined, enter TBD.

  • HE – Human Error: Operator or assembly error
  • ME – Machine Error: Equipment malfunction or calibration issue
  • MA – Material Error: Defective or off-spec material
  • DE – Design Error: Design specification issue
  • VE – Vendor Error: Supplier-related issue
  • PC – Process Control: Non-adherence to established process
  • UD – Undetermined: Cause cannot be determined at this time
  • OT – Other: Cause does not fit above categories

✅ Disposition

Select the disposition that describes the corrective action to be taken for the non-conforming item(s).

Common dispositions include:

  • Rework – Item will be corrected to meet specifications
  • Scrap – Item will be destroyed or recycled
  • Return to Vendor – Item will be sent back to the supplier
  • Use-As-Is – Item will be approved for use without correction
  • Repair – Item will be repaired to meet specifications

🔐 Verification & Closeout

Document the verification activities completed to confirm that the NCM has been resolved and the corrective action was effective.

  • For Returns: Enter the return authorization number or tracking number including the prefixed zeros
  • For Rework: Enter the work order (WO) number or rework reference
  • For Inspection: Document the inspection results or audit trail
  • For Scrap: Enter the scrap authorization or batch number

Verification must include evidence that the issue has been resolved and preventive measures are in place to avoid recurrence.
